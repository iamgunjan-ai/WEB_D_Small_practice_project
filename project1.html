A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned India scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he emerged from humble beginnings to become a leading figure in India's space and missile programs. Kalam's contributions to India's aerospace industry were monumental. He played a pivotal role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests. His visionary leadership led to the evolution of India as a self-reliant space power.

Biography

  • Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Kalam pursued a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Kalam's contributions were instrumental in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle).
  • He later transitioned to DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation) and spearheaded India's missile development program.
  • Post-presidency, Kalam continued to inspire through his books and lectures, emphasizing education, innovation, and the pursuit of excellence.
